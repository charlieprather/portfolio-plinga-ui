Plinga Gaming
UI Update

Situation

The in-game "button bar" is old and is having a few problems. Each game on Plinga's platform has a different button bar design and this makes things complicated for our Devs, especially when we release a new game. The button bar is also not up to date with design trends regarding aesthetics and functionality.

Feedback

We have decided to explore ideas that are primarily white, with lighter colors. Also, we have started discussing the idea of different options when it comes to interaction with the iframe itself. Should it push the whole things down, lay on top of it, or be permanently fixed on the top? These are important questions because various platforms that have our games, have UI elements that we don't want to disrupt.

Finish

Growth

The biggest challenge that I came across working on this project was making something that was universal for our clients but still subtly representing Plinga. A big challenege the team experienced was, once we had a design that we wanted to move forward with, we had to determin if we wanted to use SVGs for the icons, did we want to use Font Awesome, or pngs?

Some key takeaways were deepening my understanding of how jQuery works with animations and also working on a design that is universally accessible to people regardless of age or language.

I learned quite a bit from this project, mostly regarding how to work with the needs of a plethora of clients, and how to design something that is universal for so many platforms' supporting our games.
